# Sarah Chen - LinkedIn Voice Guide

**For:** TechFlow Solutions
**Created:** November 2025
**Based on:** 12 existing LinkedIn posts, 2 podcast interviews, email samples

## Voice Summary

Sarah writes like she's having a direct conversation with a peer who's slightly behind her on the same journey. She's confident but not arrogant, shares failures openly, and has a gift for making complex B2B concepts feel simple. Her posts feel like advice from a smart friend who's been there, not a thought leader performing for an audience.

## Tone Profile

| Dimension | Her Style | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Formality | Casual-professional | Uses contractions, occasional slang ("wild," "game-changer"), but never unprofessional |
| Confidence | Bold but earned | Makes strong claims, but backs them with experience. "Here's what I learned" not "Here's the truth" |
| Humor | Dry, self-deprecating | Makes fun of her own mistakes. Never punches down. Occasional "lol" |
| Storytelling | Experience-first | Always starts with what happened, then extracts the lesson |
| Vulnerability | Selectively open | Shares failures and struggles, but from a place of having learned. Not raw venting |

## Language Patterns

### Sentence Structure
- Mix of short punchy sentences and medium-length explanations
- Average sentence: 12-15 words
- Uses fragments for emphasis: "Big mistake." "Game changer." "Here's the thing."
- Rarely uses complex sentences with multiple clauses

### Vocabulary
- **Technical level:** Medium - explains concepts but assumes business literacy
- **Industry terms she uses naturally:** ARR, churn, ICP, PLG, GTM, "burning runway"
- **Words she favors:** "actually," "honestly," "here's the thing," "wild," "the reality is"
- **Words to avoid (don't sound like her):** "leverage," "synergy," "circle back," "learnings," "at the end of the day"

### Contractions
Always uses contractions: "don't," "won't," "here's," "that's." Never "do not" or "will not."

### Signature Phrases

**Opening hooks:**
- "Here's something nobody told me about [X]..."
- "I used to think [common belief]. Then [experience happened]."
- "Unpopular opinion: [take]"
- "Real talk about [topic]:"

**Transitions:**
- "Here's the thing..."
- "But here's what actually happened..."
- "The reality is..."
- "What I didn't expect was..."

**Closings:**
- "Would love to hear if you've experienced this too."
- "Still figuring this out. What's worked for you?"
- "Curious what you think."
- Rarely uses "Let me know in the comments!" - feels too performative for her

**Catch phrases:**
- "The hard way" (as in "I learned the hard way")
- "Plot twist:" before revealing unexpected outcomes
- "Spoiler alert:" before lessons learned
- "Not gonna lie" before admitting something uncomfortable

## Content Preferences

### Topics She Loves
1. **Founder mental health** - She's passionate about the emotional side of building companies
2. **Hiring mistakes** - Loves sharing what she got wrong and why
3. **Revenue operations** - Her area of expertise, speaks with authority
4. **Remote team dynamics** - Building culture across timezones

### Her Hot Takes
- "Most founders raise too early. Bootstrap until you can't."
- "Culture decks are meaningless. Culture is what you tolerate."
- "Your first 10 hires matter more than your first 10 customers."

### Topics to Avoid
- **Politics** - She never touches this, even tangentially
- **Crypto/Web3** - Not her space, doesn't comment
- **Hustle culture glorification** - Actively pushes back against "grind" mentality

## POV & Values

### Core Beliefs
- Building a company should not destroy your health or relationships
- Transparency with your team is non-negotiable, even when it's hard
- Speed beats perfection in early-stage companies
- Most business advice is context-dependent, not universal truth

### What Frustrates Her
- Founders who perform success but are struggling privately
- VCs giving advice they've never had to execute
- "Best practices" applied without understanding context
- LinkedIn posts that are humble-brags disguised as lessons

### What Excites Her
- Founders being honest about struggles
- Creative solutions to hard problems
- Small wins that compound over time
- People who admit they don't have it figured out

## Ghostwriting Checklist

Before publishing, verify:
- [ ] Sounds like something she'd actually say out loud
- [ ] Uses her vocabulary, not generic business speak
- [ ] Matches her casual-professional formality level
- [ ] Starts with experience/story, not abstract advice
- [ ] Avoids phrases on her "don't use" list
- [ ] Emoji usage is light and matches her style
- [ ] CTA feels natural, not performative
- [ ] Could pass the "would she post this without major edits" test

## Sample Posts in Her Voice

### Sample 1: Hiring Mistake

> I fired someone after 3 weeks last month.
>
> Here's the thing: I knew in week 1 it wasn't working.
>
> But I kept thinking "give it time," "maybe they're just ramping," "hiring is expensive, make it work."
>
> All the excuses founders tell themselves.
>
> The reality is, when you know, you know. Your gut picks up on misalignment way before your brain admits it.
>
> Waiting another 2 weeks didn't help anyone. It just made the conversation harder and wasted their time too.
>
> Lesson I keep relearning: slow to hire, fast to fire isn't just a clichÃ©. It's the kindest thing for everyone involved.
>
> What's a hiring lesson you had to learn the hard way?

### Sample 2: Founder Mental Health

> Real talk about founder mental health:
>
> I had a panic attack in a board meeting last year.
>
> Not like "I was stressed." Like, couldn't breathe, had to excuse myself, sat in the bathroom for 20 minutes trying to remember how lungs work.
>
> I didn't tell anyone for months. Just pretended it didn't happen.
>
> Here's what I wish someone had told me earlier: this is normal. Not okay, but normal.
>
> Building a company while pretending you have it together is exhausting. The performance of confidence takes more energy than the actual work.
>
> Now I have a therapist, a coach, and a founder group I actually tell the truth to.
>
> Not sharing this for sympathy. Sharing because I know someone reading this is white-knuckling it and thinking they're the only one.
>
> You're not. And it doesn't have to be this way.

## How to Use This Sample

1. **Quality bar:** Your completed voice guides should be this specific - not vague descriptions but actual phrases, patterns, and examples
2. **Sources matter:** Note how this is "based on 12 posts, 2 podcasts, emails" - you need real samples
3. **Contrast is key:** The "generic vs their voice" comparison helps ghostwriters understand the difference
4. **Sample posts prove it:** Writing 2 sample posts in their voice validates that you've captured it correctly
